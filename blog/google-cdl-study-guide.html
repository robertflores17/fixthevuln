Google Cloud Digital Leader Study Guide: Everything You Need to Pass the CDL Exam

Expert cybersecurity insights for IT professionals

Last updated: March 1, 2026

By FixTheVuln Team · Peer-reviewed security content · Sources: Industry frameworks and standards

The Google Cloud Digital Leader certification validates your expertise and opens doors to higher-paying roles in IT and cybersecurity. Whether you are just starting your study journey or doing a final review, this guide breaks down everything you need to know to pass the CDL exam.

Exam Overview

  • Certification: Google Cloud Digital Leader
  • Exam Code: CDL
  • Vendor: Google Cloud
  • Cost: $99 USD
  • Duration: 90 minutes
  • Questions: 50-60 questions
  • Passing Score: ~70% (scaled scoring)
  • Format: Multiple choice and multiple select
  • Prerequisites: None required; familiarity with cloud concepts recommended

Domain Breakdown

Understanding the exam domains and their weights is critical for efficient study planning. Focus more time on heavily-weighted domains while ensuring you cover all areas.

Domain 1: Digital Transformation with Google Cloud (~30%)

  • 1.1 Explain why cloud technology is transforming business
  • 1.2 Explain the benefits of cloud technology to a business
  • 1.3 Differentiate between cloud deployment models (IaaS, PaaS, SaaS)
  • 1.4 Describe the cloud adoption framework and modernization strategies

Key concepts: IaaS vs PaaS vs SaaS, Public/Private/Hybrid/Multi-Cloud, Total Cost of Ownership (TCO), OpEx vs CapEx Model, Google Cloud Adoption Framework, Digital Transformation Pillars, Sustainability and Carbon Footprint, Business Agility and Innovation

Domain 2: Innovating with Data and Google Cloud (~35%)

  • 2.1 Describe the role of data in digital transformation
  • 2.2 Identify Google Cloud data management solutions
  • 2.3 Identify Google Cloud smart analytics solutions
  • 2.4 Identify Google Cloud machine learning and AI solutions
  • 2.5 Explain the principles of responsible AI

Key concepts: BigQuery Serverless Data Warehouse, Cloud SQL and Cloud Spanner, Dataflow Stream and Batch Processing, Pub/Sub Messaging, Looker and Looker Studio Dashboards, Vertex AI Platform, AutoML and Pre-Trained APIs, Vision AI, Natural Language AI, Speech-to-Text

Domain 3: Infrastructure and Application Modernization (~35%)

  • 3.1 Explain the benefits of infrastructure modernization
  • 3.2 Differentiate between Google Cloud compute options
  • 3.3 Discuss Google Cloud networking and security
  • 3.4 Describe application modernization strategies
  • 3.5 Explain hybrid and multi-cloud strategies with Google Cloud

Key concepts: Compute Engine Virtual Machines, Google Kubernetes Engine (GKE), Cloud Run Serverless Containers, App Engine Managed Platform, Cloud Functions Event-Driven Compute, Anthos Hybrid/Multi-Cloud Platform, VPC Networks and Firewall Rules, Cloud Armor DDoS Protection

Recommended Study Timeline

Plan for approximately 4-6 weeks of dedicated study. Here is a suggested weekly breakdown:

  • Week 1: Domain 1: Digital Transformation — Why cloud, types of cloud (IaaS/PaaS/SaaS), total cost of ownership
  • Week 2: Domain 1: Cloud Value — Business agility, innovation, modernization strategies, change management
  • Week 3: Domain 2: Data & AI — BigQuery, Looker, Vertex AI, structured vs unstructured data, ML use cases
  • Week 4: Domain 2: Google Cloud AI — AutoML, pre-trained APIs (Vision, NLP, Speech), responsible AI principles
  • Week 5: Domain 3: Infrastructure — Compute Engine, GKE, Cloud Run, App Engine, hybrid/multi-cloud (Anthos)
  • Week 6: Full Review: Practice exams, Key service comparisons, Weak areas, Exam logistics

Top Study Tips

  1. Start with the official exam objectives. Download them from the Google Cloud website and use them as your study checklist. Every exam question maps to a specific objective.
  1. Use active recall over passive reading. Instead of re-reading notes, test yourself with practice questions after each study session. This dramatically improves retention.
  1. Focus on heavily-weighted domains first. Domains with higher percentages appear more on the exam. Master these before moving to lower-weighted areas.
  1. Build hands-on experience. Set up a lab environment and practice the skills you are studying. Hands-on experience is especially valuable for performance-based questions.
  1. Take practice exams under real conditions. Time yourself, eliminate distractions, and simulate the exam environment. Review every wrong answer and understand why it was wrong.
