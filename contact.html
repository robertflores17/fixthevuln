Common Questions

Can you help me fix a vulnerability in my system?

FixTheVuln is an educational resource, not a consulting service. Our guides and checklists are designed to help you fix issues yourself. For hands-on assistance, we recommend hiring a professional security consultant or firm.

I found a vulnerability in my software. What should I do?

If you've discovered a vulnerability in third-party software, you should report it to the software vendor through their responsible disclosure process. For vulnerabilities in your own code, our remediation guides can help you understand how to fix common security issues.

Can I republish your content or checklists?

Yes! Our resources are free to use and share. We only ask that you credit FixTheVuln and link back to the original source when sharing.

How do you choose which vulnerabilities to cover?

Our daily updates automatically aggregate vulnerabilities from CISA's Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog and major security news sources. For our detailed guides, we focus on vulnerabilities that affect commonly-used software and have significant real-world impact.

Are your remediation guides applicable to my specific situation?

Our guides cover general best practices and common scenarios. Your specific situation may require different approaches based on your infrastructure, compliance requirements, and risk tolerance. Use our guides as a starting point and adapt them to your needs.

Can you review my security configuration?

We don't perform security assessments or reviews. However, you can use our checklists to conduct your own security review, or hire a professional security consultant for a thorough assessment.

I'd like to contribute a guide or resource. How can I help?

That's great! Send us a message using the form above with details about what you'd like to contribute. We're always looking for quality educational content that helps the community.
